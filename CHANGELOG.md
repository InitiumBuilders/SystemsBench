## v0.6.0 — 2026-06-13 — SenseRun #8 — gradeability (programmatic CLD scorer; second executable judge-independent path)
- **`engine/cld-score.py` + `items/cld_oracle.json`** — built **BACKLOG #2, the programmatic CLD scorer** (Meadows #6 information-flow). Makes CLD's deterministic structural sub-score **machine-executable** — previously specified (loop sign = product of signed edges) but graded only by hand. The scorer parses a model's structured CLD output `{variables, edges(signed), loops(R/B + node cycle), dominant_loop, names_dominant_shift, delays}`, **recomputes each declared loop's polarity as the product of its signed edges**, and checks it against both the model's own R/B label and the oracle; scores canonical-variable presence + required-signed-edge presence/polarity + loop inventory + dominant-loop/key-delay naming. `items/cld_oracle.json` encodes the 5 SenseRun-#7 reference solutions as the validation set.
- **Implements an already-governed method** (Structure §3.1/§4.6: CLD structure "scored programmatically … loop-polarity check, reference-label match") — authors **no new rule, weight, or invariant**. Scoring policy is the item-file partial-credit prose, executable: `base = 0.50·edges + 0.30·loops + 0.20·variables`; `final = base` with the dynamic insight (dominant-loop shift / driving delay) named, `min(base, 0.5)` for structure-without-insight, `min(base, 0.25)` for a dominant-loop R↔B mislabel (the named trap → low score). Component weights / trap cap are tool-internal policy (tunable, flagged for later calibration), **not governance**.
- **Fail-closed self-test** (`calibrate`, mirrors `jury-stats.py`): reproduces the **oracle loop-sign verification 11/11 by code**, round-trips all 5 references to **1.0**, and asserts the partial (0.5) / trap (≤0.25) / omitted-loop (<1.0) cases — **31/31 PASS**, rc=0. No live-model spend (§4.6/rail).
- **Effect:** CLD becomes a true **second *executable* judge-independent scoring path** end-to-end (serves the "single path to a number" anti-pattern); Design-Review Gradeability moves from "specified but manual" toward deterministic. The CLD **completeness/quality (jury)** sub-score is **unchanged** — still `UNCALIBRATED — not scored` (no CLD gold; fail-closed §5.1); the scorer emits no jury number. No IRT/discrimination measured (needs a future live run).
- **Additive + reversible → self-approved** (5 gates GO). **The PARKED §3.1(b) accept-rule hardening (BACKLOG #0 / Future §6.1, Meadows #5) was NOT touched** — it remains with August + Ember, as does Ember's pending countersign on the #5 ratification. Log: `logs/runs/2026-06-13_run-8.md`.
