## RESEARCH (Step 3 — prior art, already in ledger; nothing new fetched)
- Krippendorff α (ordinal, ≥3 raters) — protocol §3/§8. Reused as specified.
- KNOWN PROPERTY surfaced by this run (not new lit, but newly load-bearing here):
  α is a SET-level estimator; its expected-disagreement term De is estimated from
  the marginal distribution of all coded values. At n=1 unit De is estimated from
  the same single unit as Do ⇒ Do=De ⇒ α≡0 (or 0/0 when unanimous). And even with
  sub-criteria as units, α is dominated by category PREVALENCE, not raw agreement
  (the α low-variance / prevalence paradox). Both are textbook Krippendorff; both
  bite the per-item gate as written. No new source needed — ledger §judging suffices.
