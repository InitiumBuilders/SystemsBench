## THOUGHT PROCESS (the narrative — why this move, what we expect, what we're watching)
The benchmark's binding *additive* constraint this run was coverage, not the gate. Five of seven formats held
zero items; the whole instrument was measuring one construct family (stock-flow inference) plus a single
leverage item. The §8.1 self-audit put Coverage and Discrimination both at 2 — and Discrimination is
unreachable this run (it needs live-model response data, which a SenseRun may not spend, and it is downstream
of having items at all). So the dominant *feasible* lever was coverage.

Among the empty formats, CLD was the dominant choice for three compounding reasons. First, construct: causal
loop mapping is the only format that tests feedback-structure reasoning — loops, polarity, R/B classification,
delays — which is Dimension A's core and was tested by nothing live. Second, scoring architecture: CLD's
structural component is *deterministically* gradeable (loop sign = product of edge polarities), so it adds a
second judge-independent path the way SF did — directly answering the standing "single path to a number"
anti-pattern, and doing so *without* depending on the parked §3.1(b) jury hardening. Third, leverage
compounding: the highest-leverage additive idea on the board is the programmatic CLD scorer (BACKLOG #2,
Meadows #6), and it was infeasible for one reason only — no CLD items to score or validate against. This run
manufactures exactly those inputs. We deliberately did *not* build the scorer this run: that would be a second
lever, and it would be untested without items. One lever; the scorer is now the natural next seed.

I resisted two tempting alternatives. Seeding LEV toward its floor would compound with the existing calibration
work, but LEV is fully jury-dependent and therefore gated behind the parked structural decision — more LEV
items would just queue up UNCALIBRATED behind the council. And nudging weights (BACKLOG #7) is the parameter
trap on ourselves with no IRT data to justify it. CLD was the move that buys live measurement *now* and
unblocks the highest rung *next*.

What we expect: CLD's deterministic structural sub-score goes live immediately (5 L3 items, 5 domains, past
the §3.3 threshold); its completeness sub-score stays dark until a CLD gold set is built and cleared. What we
are watching: (1) whether the L3 discriminator — the *dominant-loop shift* / delay-driven overshoot, not mere
loop-naming — actually separates models once a live run happens (the named traps are designed to make the
obvious answer wrong); (2) the R↔B mislabel and omitted-loop trap-rates as leverage-profile signals; (3) that
the structural oracle's edge/loop encodings transfer cleanly into BACKLOG #2's scorer. What we did NOT certify,
stated plainly: no CLD score is jury-validated; no IRT discrimination is measured; the items have reference
solutions but no human-labeled gold. And the parked §3.1(b) hardening (Meadows #5) was not touched — it is
August + Ember's call, with the Phase-4 evidence brief already on their desk.
