## STATE (Step 1 — Sense)
- Coverage: 1 LEV (L3) + 5 SF (L1–L3). The bench can now emit a **deterministic** Dimension-A/D score on a real run with ZERO jury dependency (SenseRun #2 unblocked this).
- Health scan: §5 (judging protocol) and §4.6 (run protocol) both repeatedly invoke a "human-labeled calibration gold set" as the **gate** that makes any jury-graded score defensible (LEV/BRIEF/open-CLD/DYN). But that gold set is **named, never specified.** There is no protocol telling anyone: how many items, who labels, how many raters, what agreement metric & threshold gates it, how disagreements resolve, how it rotates, or what happens to a score reported *before* the gate is cleared. - Consequence: the benchmark today has exactly ONE defensible scoring path (deterministic SF). Every open/high-leverage item (the LEV/BRIEF items that actually measure leverage placement — the product's whole thesis) is **blocked behind an unspecified gate.** This is the binding constraint on the benchmark's *validity claim*, not just its coverage.

## RESEARCH (Step 3 — prior art)
- Research §I.D / §judging: Zheng et al. 2023 (MT-Bench/Chatbot Arena — judge agreement vs human ceiling); Verga et al. 2024 "Replacing Judges with Juries" (PoLL — panels of small judges beat one big judge, reduce intra-family bias); Kim et al. "Prometheus" 2024 (anchored absolute grading, reference-guided).
- Agreement metric: **Krippendorff's α** (handles >2 raters, ordinal data, missing labels) — superior to Cohen's κ for our multi-rater ordinal rubric. Report **judge–human α against the human–human α ceiling** (a judge can't beat its own gold's reliability).
- Ranking-preservation check: **Kendall's τ** of model ranking (judge-scored vs human spot-check) — a benchmark can have low per-item α yet still rank models correctly; we gate on both.
- Anti-contamination of the gold set itself: keep a **sealed** portion (never shown to any judge config) to detect judge-config overfitting to the calibration set.
- Build vs reuse: **reuse** α/τ + reference-guided + jury-of-small-models discipline; author our STLC-specific labeling protocol, anchors, and thresholds. No reinvention.

## PROPOSAL (Step 4)
- Type: new protocol doc + spec rule (process/rules-of-system fix).
- Diff:
  1. New `protocols/CALIBRATION_GOLDSET.md` — full spec: size (≥120 items, ≥30 per open format, stratified by difficulty/domain), rater design (≥3 expert raters per item, blind to model identity), label = the same A–E anchored rubric the jury uses, adjudication (≥0.667 α to accept an item into gold; below → revise anchors or retire item), the **two-threshold gate** (Krippendorff α ≥ 0.667 judge–human AND Kendall τ ≥ 0.7 on ranking vs human spot-check), a **sealed split** (20%) to catch judge overfitting, rotation cadence, and the **fail-closed rule** (if the gate is unmet, jury-graded dimensions are reported as `UNCALIBRATED — not scored`, never silently averaged in).
  2. New §5.1 in Structure binding the jury to the protocol + the fail-closed rule, and updating §4.6 step 5 / §4.2 reporting to surface calibration status on every card.
- Expected effect: every open format becomes *buildable and gateable*; the benchmark gains a second defensible scoring path (gated jury) alongside deterministic SF; Gradeability 3→5 once the gate is cleared, and — critically — scores are now **honest about what is and isn't calibrated.**
- Rollback: delete `CALIBRATION_GOLDSET.md`, revert the §5.1 / §4.6 patches.
